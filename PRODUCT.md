# AutoTech Frontend

## Register

product

## Users

**Two-sided marketplace, equal priority:**

- **Car owners** — individuals in LATAM (primarily Venezuela) who need auto parts, certified repairs, or roadside assistance but lack upfront payment capability. Price-sensitive, mobile-primary, seeking trust and transparency in a high-stakes purchase (vehicle safety + financing).
- **Workshop owners** — auto repair shops seeking certification, customer leads, and a professional digital storefront. Need simple onboarding, clear listing management, and reliable payment settlement.

## Product Purpose

AutoTech is a financed auto parts marketplace and certified workshop network. The frontend is the cockpit through which both sides interact — browsing parts, applying for financing, booking workshops, managing listings, and tracking history. Success means the interface disappears into the task: a car owner finds a part and gets financed in under 3 minutes, a workshop owner receives a qualified lead without touching a CRM.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, refined, dependable.

The frontend carries the confidence of a financial instrument panel — dark, precise, information-dense where it needs to be, quiet where it doesn't. Every interaction reinforces that the platform is trusted, audited, and serious about vehicle safety and financial responsibility.

Three words: **Authoritative. Refined. Dependable.**

## Anti-references

- **Bargain-basement marketplaces** (MercadoLibre, OLX) — cluttered grids, aggressive price badges, too much visual noise. AutoTech is premium, not discount.
- **Gaudy fintech urgency** — flashing countdown timers, "limited time" badges, confetti modals, pushy upsells. Financing is a tool, not a carnival.
- **Cold bank UIs** — sterile blue/white corporate interfaces with no warmth or automotive personality.
- **Gamer-ish dark mode** — neon gradients, cyberpunk glows, aggressive angular typography. The dark palette should feel like a luxury car interior at night, not a gaming rig.
- **AI-generated landing pages** — identical card grids, gradient text, numbered section eyebrow markers, glassmorphism as default. Every pattern in the system's absolute bans list.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust through precision** — The interface feels audited, not assembled. Consistent spacing, deliberate color placement, predictable interactions.
2. **Two-sided clarity** — Navigation, language, and data display make clear whose perspective you're in, without duplicating the whole interface.
3. **Tool, not theater** — Product UI disappears into the task. Motion conveys state, not decoration. Standard affordances, not invented ones.
4. **Accessibility as a feature** — WCAG 2.1 AA enhanced is the minimum. High contrast, keyboard navigable, reduced-motion-safe, color-blind accessible.
5. **The cockpit principle** — Dark, quiet canvas. Information is revealed by intent, not broadcast. Color is rare and therefore meaningful.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA enhanced target
- Coral primary carries red-green color blindness risk — color alone must never convey information; pair with icon, shape, or text cues
- Reduced motion variants required for all transitions and reveals
- Keyboard navigation parity with pointer interactions
- Screen-reader friendly form labels, error associations, and dynamic content announcements
